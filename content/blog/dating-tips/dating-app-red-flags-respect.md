# 5 Signs You're on a Dating App That Respects You (And 5 Red Flags)

Not all dating apps are created equal. Some are designed to connect people. Others are designed to extract money. Here's how to tell the difference — and why it matters especially for gay men.

## 🟢 5 Signs of a Respectful Dating App

### 1. Messages Are Always Free

The most basic function of a dating app is communication. If an app charges you to send or read messages, it's not a dating app — it's a messaging toll booth.

A respectful app lets you talk to your matches without limits. Always.

### 2. No "Premium" Features That Should Be Basic

Seeing who liked you? Basic. Filtering by interests? Basic. Viewing full profiles? Basic.

If these are locked behind a paywall, the app is not respecting you — it's monetizing your frustration.

### 3. No Ads Between Swipes

Dating is intimate. Having an ad for crypto or a mobile game shoved between profiles breaks the experience. A respectful app either has no ads or places them so discreetly you barely notice.

### 4. Real Verification, Not Just Email

Apps that verify profiles with photos or ID show they care about your safety. Apps that let anyone join with a throwaway email don't.

### 5. Transparent About How It Works

Can you easily find information about how matches work? Is the algorithm explained? Can you contact support and get a real response? Transparency is respect.

## 🔴 5 Red Flags You're Being Exploited

### 1. The "Daily Limit" Trap

You're having a great conversation. Then: "You've reached your daily message limit. Upgrade to Premium for unlimited messages."

This is psychological manipulation. The app deliberately limits the one thing that makes dating work — talking — to force you to pay. Delete immediately.

### 2. Fake "Someone Liked You" Notifications

You get a push: "Someone liked you!" You open the app. The profile is blurred. "Subscribe to see who liked you."

These notifications are often fake — generated by the app, not real users. They're designed to trigger FOMO and extract your money.

### 3. Prices That Change Based on Your Profile

Some apps charge different users different prices for the same Premium tier. If you're over 30? Higher price. Living in a wealthy area? Higher price. This is price discrimination, and it's predatory.

### 4. Bots and Fake Profiles Everywhere

If half your matches are pushing OnlyFans links or Telegram handles, the app isn't moderating. It's either negligent (doesn't care about your experience) or complicit (bots make the app look more active).

### 5. You Can't Delete Your Account Easily

Some apps hide the delete button behind five menus. Others make you contact support. Some keep your profile "inactive" instead of deleting it.

A respectful app lets you delete everything in one tap.
