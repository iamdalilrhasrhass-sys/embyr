Dating apps are not expensive because they need to be. They are expensive because venture capital demands growth.

**The economics of a dating app**

A typical dating app raises millions in funding. Investors expect a return. The app grows quickly, acquires users, and then monetizes them. The monetization strategy is always the same: make the free version just usable enough to get people hooked, then charge for everything that actually matters.

This is why you can swipe five times and hit a paywall. This is why your messages go unread unless you pay for a boost. This is why the app shows you attractive people who never respond — to make you think paying will unlock access to them.

There is no premium tier because there is no pressure to create one. The app's success is measured by user satisfaction, not by monthly recurring revenue.
